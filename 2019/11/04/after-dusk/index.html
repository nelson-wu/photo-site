After dusk.

4 Nov 2019

The clock ticks past midnight. A single dark smear emerges out of the grotesque slab of raw concrete, and is swallowed by the darkness. Empty coffee cups pile up in the corner of a library cubicle as someone falls asleep on their laptop keyboard.

Stripped of the innovation posters and the shiny glass facades of new engineering buildings, true nature is revealed bare by the night. The structures loom large and imposingly upon our heads, lit up by an eerie incandescent light. Inside, the computer labs never sleep, fueled by the neverending desperation of students struggling to survive past the next exam.

A faint, hazy breath is stolen away by the harsh winter wind. Tomorrow, we will line up like death row prisoners in front of the gym, to stumble into the cold, mechanical lines and lines of desks that seem to stretch into the fabric of eternity, where we are crushed and ground into a soulless mixture of meat and blood.

Life ends here.
